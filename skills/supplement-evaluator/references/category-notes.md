# Supplement Category Appraisal Notes

Reference file for the supplement-evaluator skill. Read the relevant section(s) when
evaluating supplements in those categories.

---

## Adaptogens (Ashwagandha, Rhodiola, Eleuthero, Schisandra)

**Defining characteristic:** Adaptogens are claimed to non-specifically enhance resistance
to biological, physical, and chemical stress. This broad claim makes it difficult to design
a single definitive RCT.

**Evidence appraisal notes:**
- KSM-66 and Sensoril are the two most-studied ashwagandha extracts with branded RCT data.
  Evaluate them separately; generic ashwagandha studies do not transfer directly.
- Cortisol reduction is the most commonly measured primary endpoint. Distinguish between
  studies using a stress-provocation protocol (reactive cortisol) vs. resting/trough
  cortisol (tonic cortisol). These measure different things.
- Rhodiola RCTs are generally shorter (single dose to 4 weeks), with fatigue and cognitive
  performance as primary endpoints. Chronic HPA modulation data is thinner.
- Watch for the AMS (Aging Male Symptoms) scale as an outcome — it is self-reported and
  particularly susceptible to placebo effect. Always check whether objective biomarkers
  (serum cortisol, hormone panels) corroborated self-report findings.
- Cycling rationale exists for some adaptogens (rhodiola in particular): adaptogen fatigue
  is a documented phenomenon in the older herbal medicine literature, though controlled
  RCT evidence for it is limited.

## Testosterone-Supporting Ingredients

**Critical distinctions to always make:**
1. Free testosterone vs. total testosterone — free testosterone is the bioactive fraction
   and is the more clinically meaningful endpoint. A product raising total T without
   affecting free T has limited practical significance.
2. Within normal range vs. supraphysiological — a percentage increase sounds impressive
   but context requires knowing whether subjects started with low-normal, normal, or
   deficient baseline levels. Raising T from 300 to 375 ng/dL is physiologically different
   from raising it from 600 to 750 ng/dL, even if both are "+25%."
3. LH elevation — if a supplement also raises luteinizing hormone, this is strong
   mechanistic evidence of genuine upstream HPG axis stimulation, not just a downstream
   measurement artifact.

**Aromatase inhibitors:** Supplements claiming aromatase inhibition (pomegranate,
chrysin, resveratrol) should be evaluated for clinical evidence of estradiol reduction,
not just in vitro enzyme assay data. Many natural aromatase inhibitors show in vitro
activity that does not translate to meaningful in vivo effect at supplemental doses.

**Red flags in this category:**
- Studies using only within-group significance (vs. baseline) without between-group
  comparison to placebo — this is extremely common and extremely misleading in the
  testosterone booster category.
- Studies conducted exclusively in populations with clinical hypogonadism — effects
  may not generalize to eugonadal men.
- DHT changes — raises androgenic side-effect risk profile; should always be assessed.
- Estradiol changes — critical for aromatase inhibition claims.

## Nootropics and Cognitive Enhancers

**Endpoint heterogeneity is a major challenge.** Cognitive function is tested with
dozens of different batteries across studies, making cross-study comparison difficult.
Always name the specific test used (Stroop, ADAS-Cog, CANTAB, etc.) and what cognitive
domain it measures.

**Key distinctions:**
- Acute (single-dose) cognitive effects vs. chronic (multi-week) effects — many compounds
  show acute effects that do not replicate with chronic dosing, and vice versa.
- "Improves cognition in cognitively impaired subjects" ≠ "improves cognition in
  healthy adults." These are different populations with different effect expectations.
- Self-reported mental clarity is almost always subject to strong placebo effect —
  objective computerized testing is the preferred endpoint.

**Common compounds and their appraisal status:**
- L-theanine: Acute alpha wave / cortisol blunting has objective EEG support; chronic
  cognitive effects are inconsistent across trials.
- Bacopa monnieri: Requires 8–12 weeks of chronic use; acute effects are not expected.
  Most robust evidence is for memory consolidation in older adults.
- Lion's Mane mushroom: Growing RCT base; NGF induction is the proposed mechanism but
  direct CNS evidence in humans is preliminary.
- Phosphatidylserine: Reasonable evidence for cognitive decline attenuation in older
  adults; evidence in healthy young adults is weaker.

## Sleep and Stress Compounds

**Self-report vs. objective sleep measurement:**
- Actigraphy (wrist-worn device) is an objective, validated tool — significantly more
  credible than sleep diary or questionnaire alone.
- PSG (polysomnography) is the gold standard but rarely used in supplement trials due
  to cost. When present, it is a notable strength.
- The Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI) and similar questionnaires are validated
  scales but remain self-reported; placebo effect is substantial in sleep research.

**Cortisol measurement nuance:**
- Morning cortisol reflects circadian peak — most relevant for HPA axis assessment.
- Evening cortisol is normally near-zero — changes there are less meaningful.
- Cortisol awakening response (CAR) is a sensitive marker of HPA reactivity.
- Salivary cortisol has high intra-individual variability; look for sample size
  adequate to detect HPA effects (typically N > 40 per arm).

**Placebo effect is particularly prominent in sleep and stress trials.** Always check
whether placebo arms also improved substantially — if so, between-group significance
(not just within-group) is the only valid efficacy signal.

## Cardiovascular / Lipid-Modifying Nutraceuticals

**Endpoint hierarchy:**
1. Hard cardiovascular outcomes (MI, stroke, mortality) — rarely achievable in supplement
   trials; absence is not a failing, it's a design reality.
2. Surrogate biomarkers: LDL-C, ApoB, non-HDL-C, triglycerides, hsCRP, blood pressure.
   ApoB is increasingly recognized as superior to LDL-C for cardiovascular risk.
3. Flow-mediated dilation (FMD) and other functional vascular endpoints.

**Key appraisal point:** Statistically significant reductions in LDL or ApoB are only
clinically meaningful if the absolute magnitude is sufficient to shift risk category.
A 3% reduction in LDL from a supplement rarely reaches clinical meaningfulness even if
p < 0.05. Always contextualize the absolute effect size.

**Common compounds:**
- Berberine: Among the most evidence-backed lipid-modifying nutraceuticals; direct
  comparison studies vs. statins exist. Bioavailability limitations make branded
  formulations (berberine phytosome / BerberVis) clinically relevant.
- Plant sterols: Strong, replicated evidence for LDL reduction (~10% at 2g/day).
- Red yeast rice: Contains monacolin K (chemically identical to lovastatin); efficacy
  is real but regulatory status is ambiguous.
- Omega-3 (EPA/DHA): Robust triglyceride-lowering data; cardiovascular outcome data
  is mixed and dose-dependent.

## Body Composition and Performance Ingredients

**The athlete vs. general population distinction matters.** Many performance supplement
studies use trained athletes or resistance-trained subjects. Effects do not always
generalize to untrained individuals and vice versa.

**Muscle mass endpoints:**
- Lean body mass (DEXA): Gold standard.
- Fat-free mass (BIA): Acceptable but less precise.
- Arm circumference, body weight: Weak proxies; insufficient as standalone endpoints.

**Strength endpoints:**
- 1-rep max (1RM): Valid but sensitive to learning effects; requires familiarization period.
- Isokinetic dynamometry: Objective and reproducible.
- Handgrip strength: Validated proxy for overall strength; used frequently in aging
  populations as it correlates with all-cause mortality.

**Key compounds:**
- Creatine monohydrate: Among the most replicated ergogenic compounds in the literature.
  The evidence base is exceptional. Branded alternatives (Kre-Alkalyn, HCl forms) have
  not demonstrated superiority to monohydrate in head-to-head trials.
- HMB (β-hydroxy β-methylbutyrate): Solid evidence in older adults and during caloric
  restriction; weaker evidence in trained athletes.
- Citrulline malate: Growing evidence for acute performance and pump; longer-term
  body composition data is limited.
- Essential amino acids / leucine: Well-supported for muscle protein synthesis
  stimulation, particularly in older adults.
